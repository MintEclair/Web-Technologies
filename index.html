Earliest Game Memory

I remember being 4 years old, eating pizza and sitting on the couch watching my mom play Super Mario Galaxy. I didn't have to worry about classes, or money, what people thought about me or how they perceived me. I was just sitting and watching my mom chase the little starbunny, and everything was okay.

Now I'm an adult who worries about all those things. Who has gone through almost 22 years of living and has assumedly "seen everything." I feel like I've already seen life and death. Everything the world has to offer, and I find myself dissatisfied.

"Is this it?" I ask.

"What more exists in this place?" I ask again and again.

Yet I think about my mom on the first level of Super Mario Galaxy.
How she chased those starbunnies again and again, and when she finally caught them she could explore the rest of the game's world.

I sit in my room as I type this. I think about my own dissatisfaction. I remember how easy it was when I was a kid, but I also remeber another thing. Perhaps I'm still on the first level, chasing those starbunnies. It's annoying, and grating, and they seem so close yet so far.
Maybe there's more worlds to discover, and it'll happen when it happens. For now, maybe I should enjoy chasing the starbunnies here. And I will be grateful for the day I catch them and can explore the next world.
